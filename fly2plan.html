Rockport Joins Innovate UK Future Flight Challenge Fly2Plan Project

Rockport Software is proud to participate in the Fly2Plan initiative, which has just been announced. The consortium, led by Heathrow Airport, includes operational experts from airports, airlines and air traffic management, as well as technology and academic organisations. Rockport Software complements the great strengths and expertise of the consortium members, particularly in the usage and adoption of the ACRIS Semantic Model, and we look forward to working closely with them to deliver on the objectives of this exciting initiative.

Fly to Plan

The UK airspace will look very different in the coming years, as new airborne vehicles are deployed. A vast amount of flight information is currently being produced by the aviation industry, and the volumes and complexity will only increase. These information exchanges will be crucial for the safety, security and efficiency of airborne operations. The information should be based on international standards. Emerging and future models of airspace use, for example, drones and unmanned air taxis arriving and departing from an airport, and from many other locations, may require new standards. The ACRIS Semantic Model facilitates the rapid prototyping and deployment of systems and applications to support these demanding requirements of airspace users.

Rockport is proud to be working with the members of our consortium: Altitude Angel, Cirium, Consortiq, Cranfield University, Digital Catapult, IAG, IBS, Innovate UK, NATS, Rockport Software, SITA, TEKTowr, and University of Oxford
